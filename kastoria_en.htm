 Tourist Attractions of Kastoria

The City Of Kastoria

Kastoria is a city at western Macedonia and the population is about 13.500 people. The city lies on Kastoria’s lake peninsula in an altitude of approx. 700m between the mountains Vitsi and Grammos. Kastoria offers a number of tourist attractions.

Dolco

At the traditional neighborhood of Dolco we see a large part of the old city of Kastoria. There we see the mansion of Nerantzis Aivazis (17th century) which hosts the folkloric museum that represents the everyday life of the traditional Kastorian home. At Dolco we can also find the museum of Macedonian struggle and the sartorial museum as well.

Dragon’s Cave

By doing the short lake circle, at the northern part of the peninsula, we find Dragon’s cave with its extraordinary stalactites and stalagmites andit’s four lakes as well.

Holy Mary’s, Mavriotissa, monastery

Ongoing the lake’s short circle we find the monastery of Holy Mary Mavriotissa which was built at 1081 A.D.

Prophet Helias

One other route is the one who ends at the church of Prophet Helias and St. Athanasios shelter with the great panoramic view of the lake and the city.

Panagia Koumbelidiki

At the acropolis of the Byzantine castle of Kastoria, close to the wall and its two towers, rises one of the most significant Byzantine monuments of Macedonia, the church of Panagia Koumbelidiki which was built between 9th and 10th century A.D.

Byzantine Museum

The Byzantine museum there are about 550 icons, sculptures, mosaics, wood sculptures, manuscripts and other religious items from 12th to 17th century.

Neolithic Lake Settlement

A few kilometers of the city, at Dispilio, there is the Neolithic lake settlement which was discovered a sign carved with a primitive form of written language dated back at 5260 B.C.

Archaeological Museum Of Argos Orestiko

The museum’s exhibition follows the political and cultural transformations of the central part of Orestida in four successive times between 1100 B.C. up to 300 A.D.
The Museum is open for the public every day, except Monday, from 08.30 until 15.00.

Waxwork Museum

Another great suggestion is the waxwork museum, a few kilometers of the city of Kastoria, at Mavrohori, with its folkloric representations of the traditional Kastorian wedding and the representation of the prehistoric population who lived around the lake.

St. George Of Omorfoklisia

At Omorfoklisia village we find the church of St. George who was built at the 12th century A.D. Inside the church we can see the iconic wooden statue of St. George, one of the few statues in orthodox faith.

Petrified Forest Of Nostimo

At Nostimo village at 1935 was discovered the existence of petrified logs, sea species and also plants and animals that lived in the area. The findings are dated about 20 million years ago.

Tsouka

At St. Anna village there is the Byzantine monastery of Tsouka’s archangels which is built on steep rocks ta 13th century A.D. Going down to the temple the landscape is unique. A gorge separates the temple from the waterfall at the opposite side.

Vitsi Ski Resort

25 kilometers from the city, through a beautiful route, at mount Vitsi and in an altitude of 1610-1875 meters we find the ski resort which lies in a beech tree forest.
